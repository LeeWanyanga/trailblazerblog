We predict too much for the next year and yet far too little for the next ten but we can find a balance.

Finally, after all these years AFCON has finally come back home especially in East Africa.

Recently Kenya, Tanzania and Uganda put aside their differences and placed a bid as one dubbes the 'Pamoja bid' that saw them eventually win the bid to host in 2026.

AFCON typically takes place every two years, although there have been variations in the tournament schedule over the years. The competition features national teams from across Africa, which compete for the coveted title of African champions.

Senegal Midfielder, Cheikhou Kouyate receiving the trophy.

How AFCON has been over the years

The Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) is the premier international football tournament in Africa, showcasing the best talent from the continent. Organized by the Confederation of African Football (CAF), AFCON has a rich history dating back to its inaugural edition in 1957.

The tournament begins with a qualification phase, where teams compete in regional qualifiers to earn a spot in the final tournament. The final tournament usually consists of 24 teams, although the number of participating teams has varied in the past

AFCON matches are characterized by their intensity, skill, and passion, with fans from across the continent rallying behind their respective teams. The tournament culminates in the final match, where the two best teams compete for the AFCON title and the chance to etch their names into African football history

What holds for the Future

With the upcoming competition to be held in Ivory Coast, the 'Pamoja bid' countries will take notes on what to improve in order to make the competiton the best one yet.
